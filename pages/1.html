Authoritative Indian “Ground Truth” Websites

Website Purpose (What it actually is) When / How you use it
India Code
https://www.indiacode.nic.in 		Authoritative, consolidated text of Indian laws as currently in force (Acts, Rules, Subordinate Legislation). Primary spec-like source. Read the actual law: sections, provisos, amendments. Start here before blogs or commentary.
e-Gazette of India
https://egazette.gov.in 		Official publication of Notifications, Rules, amendments, commencements, appointments. Verify whether something has legally come into force. Announcements mean nothing without Gazette publication.
Constitution of India
https://legislative.gov.in/constitution-of-india 		Foundational legal document; source of authority for all laws. Use when questioning government power, fundamental rights, or constitutional validity.
Income Tax Department
https://www.incometaxindia.gov.in 		Administrative authority for Direct Taxes (CBDT circulars, instructions, forms). After reading the Act and Rules, check how the department interprets and enforces the law.
CBIC (GST & Customs)
https://www.cbic.gov.in 		Administrative authority for Indirect Taxes (GST, Customs). Reference GST circulars and notifications; binding on tax officers.
Supreme Court of India
https://www.sci.gov.in 		Final interpreter of law in India. Use when the law is ambiguous and has been settled judicially.
High Court Websites
(State-specific) 		Binding interpretations within state jurisdiction. Important where the Supreme Court has not ruled yet. Applies locally.
Ministry of Law & Justice
https://legislative.gov.in 		Draft bills, amendment acts, legal framework creation. Track future or proposed changes before they appear in India Code.
Press Information Bureau (PIB)
https://pib.gov.in 		Official government announcements (not law). Understand intent and narrative only. Always verify via Gazette.
Reserve Bank of India
https://www.rbi.org.in 		Authoritative regulator for banking and monetary policy. Read Master Directions and circulars; always trace authority back to the RBI Act.
